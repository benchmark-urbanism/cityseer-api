\section{Estimator Comparison}
\label{sec:appendix_estimator}

We selected the H\'{a}jek estimator over the Horvitz-Thompson (H-T) estimator based on theoretical and empirical considerations. The H-T estimator computes population totals as $\hat{T}_{HT} = \sum_{i \in S} y_i / \pi_i$, while the H\'{a}jek estimator uses a ratio form:
\begin{equation}
    \hat{T}_{H} = N \cdot \frac{\sum_{i \in S} y_i / \pi_i}{\sum_{i \in S} 1 / \pi_i}
\end{equation}
where $N$ is the known population size and $\pi_i$ is the inclusion probability.

The H\'{a}jek estimator exhibits lower variance when the population size is known and sample values correlate with inclusion probabilities---both conditions satisfied in our setting. Empirical comparison across synthetic networks confirmed that the H\'{a}jek estimator consistently achieved equal or higher accuracy.

\section{Algorithm Pseudocode}
\label{sec:appendix_algorithm}

The adaptive sampling procedure operates in three phases:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Probe phase:} Sample $n_{\text{probe}} = f_{\text{probe}} \cdot |V|$ nodes uniformly at random. Compute centralities from these nodes to estimate mean reachability $\bar{R}_d$ at each distance threshold $d$.

    \item \textbf{Calibration phase:} For each distance $d$:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Look up required \effn{} for target $\rho$ from the appropriate empirical model (closeness or betweenness; shortest or angular)
        \item Compute sampling probability: $p_d = \effn / \bar{R}_d$, clamped to $[0, 1]$
    \end{itemize}

    \item \textbf{Sampling phase:} For each node, include if $\text{rand}() < \max_d(p_d)$. Run bounded Dijkstra from sampled nodes and apply the H\'{a}jek estimator with distance-specific weights $w_d = p_d / \max_d(p_d)$.
\end{enumerate}

The computational complexity is $O((f_{\text{probe}} + p_{\max}) \cdot |V| \cdot (|V| + |E|) \log |V|)$, yielding speedup factor $\approx 1 / (f_{\text{probe}} + p_{\max})$ when $p_{\max} < 1$.
