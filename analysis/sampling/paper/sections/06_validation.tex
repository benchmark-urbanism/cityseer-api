\subsection{Experimental Protocol}

We validate the model on the GLA and Greater Madrid networks described in Section~\ref{sec:methods}. For each distance threshold, we compute ground-truth centrality ($p = 1.0$) and sampled centrality at probabilities from 1\% to 100\%, measuring Spearman $\rho$ between the two. Each configuration is repeated with 3 random seeds; we report the mean $\pm$ standard deviation.

\subsection{GLA Results}

Figure~\ref{fig:validation} shows validation results for the Greater London network. At all three distances, the model-recommended probability achieves $\rho > \glaMinRho$, exceeding the \targetRho{} target. Observed $\rho$ consistently exceeds model predictions, demonstrating appropriate conservatism.

The validation demonstrates successful extrapolation well beyond synthetic training data: from $\approx 2{,}000$-node training networks to \glaNnodes{} nodes, from maximum training reach of $\approx 2{,}600$ to \glaTwentyKmReach{}, and from controlled synthetic topologies to heterogeneous real-world structure. Despite these extrapolations, predictions remain conservative, suggesting that the $\effn$-accuracy relationship is a robust property of network centrality rather than an artifact of specific configurations.

\subsection{External Validation: Madrid}

To test generalisability beyond London, we applied the identical protocol to the Greater Madrid metropolitan street network. The model achieves $\rho > \targetRho$ for both closeness and betweenness at all tested distances (Figure~\ref{fig:madrid_validation}), confirming that the fitted parameters generalise across morphologically distinct European cities.
