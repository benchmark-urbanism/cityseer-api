\subsection{Applying the Model}

For users of the \cityseer{} library, the adaptive sampling functions implement the model automatically (see Appendix~\ref{sec:appendix_cityseer_impl}). For other frameworks, the procedure is: (1) estimate reach $r$ at each analysis distance, (2) compute $\effn = \max(\kProp \cdot \sqrt{r}, \minEffN)$ and $p = \min(1, \effn/r)$, and (3) compute centrality from a random $p$-fraction of sources, scaling results by $1/p$.

Figure~\ref{fig:guide} provides visual lookup charts for common scenarios, and Table~\ref{tab:lookup} gives a numerical reference for representative reach values.

\subsection{Rules of Thumb and Edge Cases}

As a rough guide: at reach below $\crossoverReach$, use full computation---sampling overhead outweighs savings. At reach 1,000--10,000, sample 10--30\% (3--10$\times$ speedup). At reach above 10,000, sample 5--15\% (7--20$\times$ speedup). For applications requiring higher accuracy than $\rho \geq \targetRho$, increase $\effn$ by a factor of 1.5--2$\times$ or average 2--3 replications with different random seeds.

Sampling is not appropriate when exact centrality values (not approximations) are required, when centrality is needed for specific nodes rather than the full network, or when the network is small enough ($< 1{,}000$ nodes) that full computation is already fast.
